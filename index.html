EXERCISE OF THE WEEK

Day 1: Upper Body Strength
  • Bench Press: 3 sets of 8-10 reps
  • Bent-Over Rows: 3 sets of 8-10 reps
  • Overhead Shoulder Press: 3 sets of 8-10 reps
  • Bicep Curls: 3 sets of 10-12 reps
  • Tricep Dips: 3 sets of 10-12 reps
  • 10-15 minutes of cardio as a warm-up
Day 2: Lower Body Strength
  • Squats: 3 sets of 10-12 reps
  • Deadlifts: 3 sets of 8-10 reps
  • Lunges: 3 sets of 12 reps (each leg)
  • Leg Press: 3 sets of 10-12 reps
  • Calf Raises: 3 sets of 12-15 reps
  • 10-15 minutes of cardio as a warm-up
Day 3: Cardio and Core
  • 30-45 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio (running, cycling, elliptical, etc.)
  • Planks: 3 sets, hold for 30-45 seconds each
  • Russian Twists: 3 sets of 20 twists
  • Leg Raises: 3 sets of 15 reps
  • Bicycle Crunches: 3 sets of 20 reps (each side)
Day 4: Rest and Recovery
Day 5: Total Body Strength
  • Pull-Ups or Lat Pulldowns: 3 sets of 8-10 reps
  • Push-Ups or Chest Press Machine: 3 sets of 10-12 reps
  • Leg Extensions: 3 sets of 10-12 reps
  • Leg Extensions: 3 sets of 10-12 reps
  • Dumbbell Shoulder Press: 3 sets of 10-12 reps
  • 10-15 minutes of cardio as a warm-up
Day 6: Cardio and Conditioning
  • 30 minutes of high-intensity interval training (HIIT) on the treadmill, bike, or rowing machine
  • Incorporate 30 seconds of intense effort followed by 1 minute of rest
  • Burpees, mountain climbers, and jumping jacks can be included in the routine
Day 7: Active Recovery or Rest
  • Engage in light activities like walking, gentle yoga, or stretching to promote recovery and flexibility Remember to adjust the weights, sets, and repetitions based on your fitness level and progression. Stay consistent, stay hydrated, and ensure you're consuming a balanced diet to support your workouts and recovery.
